### 4.1 Issue Priority Definitions

**Critical (P1):**
- Software completely non-functional
- Severe data loss or corruption
- Security vulnerability being exploited
- Business operations fully stopped

**High (P2):**
- Major functionality not working
- Migration failures affecting business operations
- Significant performance degradation
- Workaround not available

**Medium (P3):**
- Feature not working as documented
- Performance issues with workaround available
- Non-critical functionality impaired
- Minor bugs affecting usability

**Low (P4):**
- Cosmetic issues
- Documentation errors or clarifications
- Feature requests
- General questions

### 4.2 Response Time Definitions

**Response Time:**
Time from ticket creation to initial acknowledgment and assignment to support specialist.

**Resolution Time:**
Time from ticket creation to issue resolution. Not guaranteed; varies by complexity.

**Target Resolution Times (Best Effort):**
- Critical: 24 hours or workaround provided
- High: 72 hours or workaround provided
- Medium: 5 business days
- Low: 10 business days or next release

### 4.3 Response Time Exceptions

Response times may be extended due to:
- Information gaps (awaiting customer response)
- Need for customer environment access
- Third-party vendor dependencies
- Complexity requiring engineering escalation
- Force majeure events

Customers will be notified of delays with revised ETAs.

## 6. Support Process and Procedures

### 6.1 Contacting Support

**Email Support:**

- Premium: premium-support@oursystemadmin.com
- Emergency: emergency@oursystemadmin.com

**Web Portal:**

- Account management

**Phone Support (Premium Tier):**
- Direct phone number provided to Premium customers
- Available during extended support hours
- Faster escalation for urgent issues

### 6.2 Information to Provide

To expedite support, please provide:

**Required Information:**
- Software version number (Help > About)
- Operating system and version
- QuickBooks version being used
- License key or registered email address
- Description of the issue
- Error messages (exact wording or screenshots)
- Steps to reproduce the problem

**Helpful Additional Information:**
- Company File size
- When issue first occurred
- Recent changes to system or Software
- Whether issue is reproducible
- Migration logs (if applicable)

### 6.3 Support Ticket Lifecycle

**1. Ticket Creation**
- Customer submits issue via email, portal, or phone
- Automatic acknowledgment sent immediately
- Ticket number assigned for tracking

**2. Initial Review**
- Support specialist reviews and prioritizes
- May request additional information
- Assigns priority level

**3. Troubleshooting**
- Support specialist investigates issue
- May request logs, screenshots, or remote access
- Tests potential solutions
- Regular status updates provided

**4. Resolution**
- Solution identified and communicated
- Customer implements solution with guidance
- Verification that issue is resolved

**5. Closure**
- Customer confirms resolution
- Ticket closed with resolution notes
- Customer satisfaction survey sent

**6. Follow-Up**
- Check-in after 48 hours
- Confirmation that solution remains effective
- Offer additional assistance if needed

### 6.4 Escalation Procedures

**When to Escalate:**
- Issue not resolved within target timeframe
- Customer dissatisfaction with support quality
- Complex technical issues requiring engineering
- Billing or licensing disputes
- Ethical or legal concerns

**Escalation Path:**
- Level 1: Frontline Support Specialist
- Level 2: Senior Support Specialist
- Level 3: Support Manager
- Level 4: Engineering Team
- Level 5: Director of Support
- Level 6: Executive Management

**How to Request Escalation:**
- Email: escalation@oursystemadmin.com
- Mention escalation request in ticket
- Contact support manager directly
- Request callback from supervisor

**Escalation Response:**
- Manager review within 4 hours
- Direct contact from escalation level
- New estimated resolution timeline
- Enhanced attention and resources

## 7. Remote Support Services

### 7.1 When Remote Access is Offered

Remote support sessions may be provided for:
- Complex troubleshooting requiring screen observation
- Installation or configuration assistance
- Performance optimization
- Training on advanced features
- Migration supervision for large or critical files

### 7.2 Remote Access Procedures

**Authorization:**
- Explicit customer consent required for each session
- Documented approval in ticket
- Right to decline or terminate session at any time
- Session recording for quality assurance (with notice)

**Security:**
- Secure, encrypted remote access tools
- Session-based access (no persistent connections)
- View-only or limited control options
- Customer monitors all activities
- No access outside of session

**Privacy:**
- Support specialist only views information necessary to resolve issue
- Confidentiality of all observed information
- No screenshots or recording by support specialist (except with permission)
- Memory of session not retained after completion

### 7.3 Customer Responsibilities During Remote Session

**Before Session:**
- Close unrelated applications
- Ensure sensitive information not visible on screen
- Have relevant files and information ready
- Backup critical data

**During Session:**
- Remain present and attentive
- Follow support specialist's instructions
- Ask questions if anything unclear
- Do not perform other tasks simultaneously

**After Session:**
- Verify issue resolution
- Review any changes made
- Test functionality
- Revoke remote access (if applicable)

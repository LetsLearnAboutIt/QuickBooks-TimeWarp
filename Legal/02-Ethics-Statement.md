## 🔒 Data Privacy Is Our Core Ethical Principle

We built QuickBooks Time-Warp® on one uncompromising principle: **your data is yours, and yours alone.**

We engineered our software to run **100% locally on your computer** precisely because we believe no vendor should ever need to see, touch, or hold your financial data. We don't collect it. We don't transmit it. We don't store it. We don't even have the ability to access it.

This isn't just a feature — it's an ethical commitment.

## 2. Data Confidentiality

### 2.1 Absolute Confidentiality
We recognize that QuickBooks data contains sensitive financial information that is critical to our clients' businesses. We pledge:

- **Never to access client data** without explicit written permission
- **Never to disclose or share** client financial information with third parties
- **To maintain strict confidentiality** even after service relationships end
- **To protect data** as if it were our own most sensitive information

### 2.2 No Unauthorized Access
Our Software is designed with privacy-by-design principles:
- No remote access capabilities without explicit authorization
- No backdoors or hidden data collection mechanisms
- No transmission of client data to our servers
- No analytics or telemetry that could compromise confidentiality

### 2.3 Employee Training
All personnel with potential access to client systems are:
- Thoroughly vetted through background checks
- Trained in data confidentiality and privacy protocols
- Bound by confidentiality agreements
- Subject to regular ethics training

## 7. Compliance with Accounting Standards

### 7.1 Respect for Accounting Principles
We design QuickBooks Time-Warp to respect and preserve:
- Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP)
- International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS)
- QuickBooks data integrity requirements
- Audit trail requirements
- Financial reporting standards

### 7.2 Data Integrity
We ensure that migration operations:
- Preserve all financial transaction data accurately
- Maintain proper date relationships and sequences
- Retain complete audit trails
- Do not alter the economic substance of transactions
- Produce verifiable and reconcilable results

### 7.3 Professional Guidance
We recommend that clients:
- Consult with their accountants before major migrations
- Verify migrated data with accounting professionals
- Maintain proper documentation of migration activities
- Understand tax implications of date adjustments
- Follow their jurisdiction's record-keeping requirements
